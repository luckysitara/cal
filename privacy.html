Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 29, 2026

Solaris ("we", "our", or "us") provides a local-first utility mobile application designed to calculate cryptocurrency exchange rates, track token balances, and monitor watched public wallet addresses on the Solana blockchain. We are committed to protecting your privacy.

1. Information We Do Not Collect

Solaris is built on a local-first architecture. This means:

  • We do not collect, transmit, upload, or store any of your personal data, wallet addresses, account balances, or private keys to our own servers.
  • We do not track your location, device identifier, or usage behavior.
  • We do not use any third-party analytics or tracker SDKs inside our application.

2. On-Device Storage

Any configuration you create within the app is stored locally on your device's persistent storage (using standard Android AsyncStorage mechanisms). This includes:

  • A list of public wallet addresses you have authorized via the Mobile Wallet Adapter (MWA) protocol.
  • Your watched wallet addresses (watch-only public keys).
  • Your manual portfolio simulation balances.
  • Your selected active token and active fiat currency settings.

You can delete this data at any time by clearing the application storage, uninstalling the app, or disconnecting wallets within the holdings tab.

3. Third-Party Web3 Service Queries

To display real-time valuation and balances, the application makes direct HTTP requests from your device to public Web3 interfaces:

  • Solana RPC Nodes: The app queries a Solana RPC endpoint (defaulting to the official Solana Mainnet Beta node `api.mainnet-beta.solana.com` or your custom configured RPC node) to fetch lamport and token account balances.
  • Jupiter API: The app queries the Jupiter Price API (`api.jup.ag`) to pull active token prices and verified token lists.
  • ExchangeRate API: The app queries `open.er-api.com` to retrieve standard USD-to-fiat conversion rates.

These third-party services may receive standard network metadata (such as your IP address) necessary to resolve the query. We do not control, and are not responsible for, the privacy practices of these third-party endpoints.

4. Security

Because we do not collect or transmit your information, the security of your data depends on securing your physical device. We recommend using device locks, biometric authentication, and maintaining security updates on your Android OS.

5. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date.

6. Contact Us

If you have any questions or feedback about this Privacy Policy, you can contact us through our official project repository.
